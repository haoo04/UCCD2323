Baguette

French Bread

  • Overview
  • Recipe

Introduction

The baguette, a long, thin loaf of French bread that is known for its crispy crust and airy crumb, is a staple of French culture and cuisine. The word "baguette" means "wand" or "baton" in French, referring to its shape. Baguettes have been a part of French daily life for centuries and are typically served fresh with butter, jam, or cheese.

The traditional baguette is made from a simple dough of flour, water, yeast, and salt. It is distinguished by its golden-brown crust, soft interior, and its characteristic scoring, which allows the bread to expand during baking. The baguette has gained international fame and is often associated with French identity.

Origin Country

France

Main Ingredients

Flour

Yeast

Salt

Ingredients

Flour 4 cups Water 1 1/2 cups
Yeast 2 teaspoons Salt 2 teaspoons

Instructions

Step 1

Make the Dough

Mix the flour, yeast, and salt in a large bowl. Gradually add water and knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rise for 1-2 hours until it doubles in size.

Step 2

Shape the Baguettes

Divide the dough into 3 equal portions. Shape each portion into a long, thin loaf, making sure to gently stretch the dough without deflating it. Place the shaped loaves on a floured baking sheet and cover them with a cloth. Let them rise for another 30 minutes.

Step 3

Score and Bake

Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). Using a sharp knife, make diagonal slashes along the top of each baguette. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until the crust is golden brown and the loaves sound hollow when tapped. Allow the baguettes to cool before serving.

Step 4

Serve

Enjoy your freshly baked baguette with butter, cheese, or your favorite spread. Baguettes are best enjoyed fresh on the day they are baked.

Notes

  • For a crispier crust, spray water into the oven during the first 10 minutes of baking.
  • If you don’t have bread flour, all-purpose flour can be used as a substitute, though the texture may be slightly different.
  • Baguettes can be stored in a paper bag for a day or two, but they are best eaten fresh.
