Coq au Vin

French Wine-Braised Chicken

  • Overview
  • Recipe

Introduction

Coq au Vin is a classic French dish where chicken is braised with wine, mushrooms, bacon, and garlic. The name "Coq au Vin" translates to "rooster in wine," reflecting the dish's rustic origins. Traditionally, this dish was made with an older, tougher bird and slow-cooked in wine to tenderize the meat. Today, it is commonly made with chicken and is a staple in French cuisine, often served with potatoes or crusty bread.

The dish is known for its deep flavors, achieved by marinating the chicken in red wine, then slow-cooking it with aromatic vegetables and herbs. The resulting sauce is rich and flavorful, perfectly complementing the tender chicken.

Origin Country

France

Main Ingredients

Chicken

Red Wine

Mushrooms

Bacon

Garlic

Herbs

Ingredients

Chicken 1 whole, cut into pieces Red Wine 1 bottle (750 ml)
Bacon 100g, diced Mushrooms 200g, sliced
Garlic 4 cloves, minced Onion 1 large, chopped
Carrots 2, sliced Thyme 2 sprigs
Bay Leaf 1 Butter 2 tablespoons
Flour 2 tablespoons Salt to taste
Black Pepper to taste Chicken Stock 1 cup

Instructions

Step 1

Marinate the Chicken

In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with red wine, garlic, thyme, and bay leaf. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse.

Step 2

Cook the Bacon and Vegetables

In a large pot or Dutch oven, cook the diced bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and set aside. In the same pot, add the butter and sauté the onions, garlic, and carrots until softened.

Step 3

Brown the Chicken

Remove the chicken from the marinade (reserve the marinade) and pat dry. Dredge the chicken pieces in flour and brown them in the pot with the vegetables. Once browned, remove the chicken and set aside.

Step 4

Simmer the Chicken

Return the chicken and bacon to the pot. Pour in the reserved marinade and chicken stock. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours, until the chicken is tender.

Step 5

Add Mushrooms

In the final 15 minutes of cooking, add the sliced mushrooms to the pot. Continue to simmer until the mushrooms are tender and the sauce has thickened.

Step 6

Serve

Serve the Coq au Vin hot, garnished with fresh parsley. It pairs wonderfully with mashed potatoes, crusty bread, or buttered noodles.

Notes

  • You can use either a young chicken or an older bird, depending on your preference. Older birds may require longer cooking times.
  • For a richer flavor, consider using a Burgundy or Pinot Noir wine.
  • Coq au Vin is even better the next day, as the flavors have more time to meld together. Reheat gently on the stove before serving.
