Countries' Highlight

Nasi Lemak

Nasi lemak is a rice dish commonly served with roasted nuts, egg, ikan bilis (anchovies), and slices of cucumber. Literally meaning “fatty rice” in Malay, nasi lemak’s distinctive taste comes from cooking the rice in coconut milk and pandan leaves which gives the dish its rich flavour and fragrant aroma.It is rounded off with spicy sambal – a chilli paste made from dried chilies, garlic, shallots, and belacan (shrimp paste).

Paella

Paella (pai·ei·uh) is a classic Spanish rice dish made with rice, saffron, vegetables, chicken, and seafood cooked and served in one pan. Although paella originates from Valencia, it’s recognized as the national food of Spain and there are several different varieties. The most common types of paella are chicken paella, seafood paella, or mixed paella (a combination of seafood, meats, and vegetables).

Shoyu Ramen

Shoyu ramen is a Japanese noodle soup featuring a soy sauce-based broth. It typically includes thin, curly noodles and is garnished with toppings such as sliced pork (chashu), green onions, bamboo shoots, nori (seaweed), and sometimes a soft-boiled egg. The soy sauce imparts a savory and slightly salty flavor, making it distinct and widely enjoyed in various regional styles across Japan.

Pepperoni Pizza

Pepperoni pizza is a popular type of pizza topped with slices of pepperoni, a spicy Italian-American variety of salami. It typically features a tomato sauce base and is covered with mozzarella cheese before the pepperoni slices are added. The pizza is then baked until the crust is crispy, the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the pepperoni becomes slightly crispy and flavorful. Pepperoni pizza is known for its rich, savory taste and is a staple in pizzerias worldwide.

Fish and Chips

Fish and chips is a classic British dish consisting of battered and deep-fried fish, typically cod or haddock, served with thick-cut fried potatoes (chips). The fish is coated in a light, crispy batter and fried until golden brown, while the chips are soft on the inside and crispy on the outside. It is often accompanied by salt, vinegar, and sometimes tartar sauce or mushy peas. This dish is a staple of British cuisine and is commonly enjoyed as a takeaway meal.

Middle East
Middle Eastern cuisine, encompassing countries like Lebanon, Turkey, Iran, and Israel, is celebrated for its flavorful use of spices and fresh ingredients. It includes dishes such as kebabs, hummus, falafel, and tabbouleh.
Europe
European cuisine is diverse and rich, featuring fresh, high-quality ingredients such as herbs, cheeses, meats, and grains. It includes various cooking methods like roasting, baking, sautéing, and braising.
Southeast Asia
Southeast Asian cuisine, including from Thailand, Vietnam, Malaysia, Singapore, and Indonesia, is known for its spicy, tangy, sweet, and savory flavors. It uses fresh seafood, herbs, and vegetables, with cooking methods like grilling, stir-frying, and steaming.
East Asia
East Asian cuisine, from China, Japan, South Korea, North Korea, and Mongolia, balances sweet, sour, salty, and umami flavors. It highlights fresh ingredients like seafood, vegetables, and rice, with cooking methods including stir-frying, steaming, and grilling.
Arfica
African cuisine, from countries like Egypt, Morocco, Nigeria, and Ethiopia, features rich flavors with a focus on spices, herbs, and diverse ingredients. Common elements include grains like couscous, beans, and vegetables.
