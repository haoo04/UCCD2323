Mediterranean Bowl

Healthy Mediterranean Delight

  • Overview
  • Recipe

Introduction

The Mediterranean Bowl is a vibrant and nutritious dish with origins in Greece, inspired by the diverse and rich flavors of the Mediterranean region. This bowl combines a variety of fresh vegetables, grains, and proteins to create a balanced and flavorful meal.

Typically, a Mediterranean Bowl features ingredients such as creamy hummus, crispy falafel, crunchy cucumbers, juicy tomatoes, and briny olives. Fresh herbs and spices, like parsley, mint, and cumin, are added to enhance the dish's aromatic and vibrant flavors.

Traditionally, it is served with a generous drizzle of high-quality olive oil and a sprinkle of tangy feta cheese, which add depth and richness to the bowl. This combination makes the Mediterranean Bowl a perfect choice for a healthy lunch or dinner, offering a delicious taste of Greek culinary traditions while highlighting the best of Mediterranean cuisine.

Origin Country

Greece

Main Ingredients

Hummus

Falafel

Cucumber

Tomato

Olive

Feta Cheese

Ingredients

Hummus 1/2 cup Falafel 1 cup
Cucumber 1, sliced Tomato 1, diced
Olives 1/4 cup, sliced Feta Cheese 1/4 cup, crumbled
Olive Oil 2 tablespoons Lemon Juice 1 tablespoon
Fresh Herbs 1/4 cup, chopped Salt to taste

Instructions

Step 1

Prepare Ingredients

Prepare all the ingredients by slicing the cucumber, dicing the tomato, and slicing the olives. Crumble the feta cheese and chop the fresh herbs.

Step 2

Assemble the Bowl

In a bowl, place a portion of hummus as the base. Add falafel, cucumber, tomato, olives, and feta cheese on top. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice.

Step 3

Garnish

Sprinkle chopped fresh herbs over the top and season with salt to taste. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • You can substitute the falafel with grilled chicken or tofu for a different protein option.
  • Feel free to add other vegetables like bell peppers or red onions for extra crunch and flavor.
  • For a spicier kick, add a sprinkle of chili flakes or a dollop of harissa.
  • The Mediterranean Bowl can be served warm or cold, making it a versatile dish for any time of year.
